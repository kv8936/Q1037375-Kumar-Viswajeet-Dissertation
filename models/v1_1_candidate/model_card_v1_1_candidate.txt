
MODEL CARD: Workplace Hazard AI Chatbot - v1.1 Candidate

Project:
Assessing the Accuracy, Usability, and Decision-Support Capability of an AI-powered Chatbot
for Workplace Hazard Identification and Risk Assessment.

Version:
v1_1_candidate

Purpose:
This model version is designed to support workplace hazard identification and early-stage
risk assessment in a controlled dissertation prototype. It is not designed to replace
professional workplace safety inspection or legal compliance assessment.

Dataset:
- Total rows: 360
- Hazard categories: 6
- Risk levels: Low, Medium, High
- Split: 252 train, 54 validation, 54 test
- Stratification: hazard_category + risk_level

Architecture:
1. Hazard category classification:
   - Method: TF-IDF + Logistic Regression
   - Validation accuracy: 0.9259
   - Test accuracy: 0.8889

2. Risk level classification:
   - Method: deterministic severity_score rule
   - Rule:
     Low = severity_score 2–3
     Medium = severity_score 4–6
     High = severity_score 7–9
   - Validation accuracy: 1.0000
   - Test accuracy: 1.0000
   - High → Low test errors: 0

Combined Manual Review Safety Control:
The chatbot flags a case for manual review if either condition is met:

1. Confidence rule:
   hazard_confidence < 0.40

2. Ambiguity rule:
   fire/exit route terms and obstruction terms are both detected.

Test-set manual-review performance:
- Test cases flagged for manual review: 30 out of 54
- Flagged percentage: 55.56%
- Hazard errors caught by combined review flag: 6
- Hazard errors missed by combined review flag: 0

Known Limitations:
- Hazard classification still shows confusion between overlapping workplace categories,
  especially Fire, Obstruction, Visibility, and Electrical hazards.
- The combined manual-review rule catches all observed hazard errors in the test set, but it flags
  more than half of test cases, increasing human review workload.
- Risk classification depends on the availability and correctness of severity_score.
- The dataset is synthetic and should be interpreted as prototype evidence, not operational proof.
- The ambiguity rule is domain-specific and was added after error analysis of fire-exit obstruction cases.

Conclusion:
v1.1_candidate improves safety and transparency by replacing the weak ML risk classifier with
a deterministic severity_score rule and adding a combined manual-review mechanism for uncertain
or ambiguous hazard predictions. The hybrid design is suitable for dissertation evaluation,
provided its limitations and human-review requirement are clearly reported.
